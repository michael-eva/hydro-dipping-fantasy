What is Hydro-dipping?

Hydro-dipping, also known as liquid-dipping, water-transfer printing and hydro-graphics, is the process of applying a printed design onto any paintable surface using water as the transfer medium. As a rule of thumb, if the item can be dipped in water and can be painted using traditional painting techniques then the item can be hydro-dipped.

How does it work?

The item first needs to be prepared before it can be hydro-dipped. The surface must be cleaned and primed and depending on the design of the film you are using, it might need a base coat before it can be hydro-dipped. Some film designs are clear which means that the base coat you choose will affect the outcome.

A container of water is needed which will be large enough to fit the object comfortably in and deep enough so that the whole item be submerged underwater.

The film needs to be cut to the correct size to ensure that no film is wasted. It is best to leave about 5cm extra on the sides. If you are starting out, it might be easier to cut a bigger piece than you need just to get used to the process.

It is important to use masking tape to mark off a box in the container around the film about 3cm bigger than the film because in the steps to follow, the film will expand and we can't let it expand too far or else it will distort the design.

The film has to be placed into the container being sure to place it the right way around. If you lick your thumb and index finger and grab onto the corner of the film, the side of the film that sticks to your finger goes face down into the water. The most effective way to put the film into the water is by grabbing one corner and the corner diagonal to it and gently lowering it into the container so that no air bubbles get trapped under the film. If air bubbles happen to get trapped, gently work them out or else it will affect the design when the item gets dipped.

The film needs to sit in the water for at least a minute so that the film can soak up the water. If you leave it for more than a minute, you will achieve the same results but if it's left for less than a minute, it might not work the way it's supposed to. When the film is ready, it will seem almost wrinkly.
