What does activator do?

I don't know the exact science behind what activator does, but the practical application is that once the film has been saturated with water, the activator liquifies the film so that you can easily dip your item.

How to make your own activator:

I have researched this quite a lot and I am aware of only one person who gives you the formula for the activator that he makes. Everybody else on eBay tries to sell you a formula. The ingredients you'll need are:

  • Paint thinners, and
  • Xylene

I managed to get them at Bunnings, the brand I got was 'DIGGERS' and it worked fine.

You'll also need:

  • Gloves,
  • A spray bottle (if you don't have a spray gun or a compressor), and
  • A measuring cup.

I tried a couple of spray bottles but the ones that seem to work the best are old spray bottles that had surface cleaner or windex in it. Be careful when spending money on a spray bottle because the activator is solvent based and it tends to melt a lot of the spraying mechanisms.

I also used an old aerosol can which worked pretty well and was a cool project to work on in my spare time.

The formula:

The formula I used and which was recommeded by a YouTube user by the name of "Keewee" was 30% xylene and 70% paint thinner - simple.

Mix those 2 together and you're ready to go. Pour the mixture into your spray bottle and you're ready to go.

I recommend using gloves when you're dealing with these chemicals because they can burn your skin.
